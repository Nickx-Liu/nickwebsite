Overview

Revolutionary War

The American Revolution (1775-1783) was the seccession of Great Britain's 13 Colonies in North America due to their lack of representation in the British Parliament, with the colonies eventually forming the United States of America after successfully gaining their independence.

War of 1812

British restrictions on US trade with France and British impressment of American Sailors during the Napoleonic Wars between Great Britain and France led to the outbreak of the War of 1812 which lasted from June 1812 to March 1815, resulting in over 2,000 American deaths.

Mexican - American War

The Mexican-American War between America and Mexico (1846-1848) stemmed from America's annexation of Texas and the dispute on where the Texas borders were; this war was the first American conflict fought on foreign land, leading to the expansion of US territory in North America.

Civil War

The Civil War (1861-1865) resulted from tensions between the northern and southern states in the US over issues regarding slavery and states' rights; the Civil War is America's deadliest war with approximately 620,000 soldiers dead as a result of the war.

Spanish - American War

Cuban struggle for independence from Spain which lasted from 1895 to 1898 had garnered much attention and sympathy from the American people, and rising tensions between the United States and Spain made American intervention very likely. The Spanish-American lasted from April to August 1898 with the Treaty of Paris ceding Puerto Rico, Guam, and the Philippines, aswell as granting Cuba their independence.
